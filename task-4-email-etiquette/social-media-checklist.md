**5 Do's and 5 Don'ts for responsible social media use as a college student**

| Do's<br />| Don'ts|
|-|-|
|Do prioritize authenticity over performance. Ensure your public profiles (like LinkedIn or GitHub) reflect actual competence rather than just a curated version of reality.|Don't post incomplete or copied work. Avoid uploading unfinished projects or copied code just to make your profile look active and proactive.|
|Do remember digital permanence. Act with the understanding that digital activity does not disappear; early mistakes or exaggerated claims remain visible to future recruiters.|Don't let casual habits blur into professional spaces. Do not assume that the informal, rapid communication style used on social media is acceptable when reaching out to professionals or faculty|
|Do verify before you click. Always check sender email addresses and verify opportunities through official websites before responding to urgent links or messages.|Don't fall for the illusion of convenience. Do not let the ease of digital tools stop you from questioning the content you consume or create.|
|Do pause and reflect on risks. Take time to consider how your communication is perceived and what risks you might be exposing yourself to before hitting "send" or "post".|Don't overshare. Avoid exposing sensitive personal information simply because a platform makes sharing frictionless and easy.|
|Do cultivate a habit of critical thinking. Approach digital literacy as a continuous habit of questioning, verifying, and taking responsibility for your actions.|Don't prioritize speed over presentation. Do not let the pressure to respond quickly override your awareness of proper structure, tone, and professional etiquette|
